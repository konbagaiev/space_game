# Character — the player (rookie Vega Sentinel)

The protagonist. First-person in the intro cutscene; addressed in second person ("Sentinel") by
mission control everywhere else. Kept deliberately thin so the player projects onto their own
**callsign** (chosen after Level 1).

## Established
- **Role:** a **Vega Sentinel** — the setting's defender corps, the game's namesake.
- **Status at the start:** a **rookie** — fresh commission, straight out of training, on the way to a
  **first posting** in a **factory-new ship**. Not a veteran; this is their first real combat.
- **Inciting moment:** **ambushed by pirates on approach** to the station — unexpected, not a briefed
  mission. They fight through it and make it to the station (→ Level 1).
- **Title & identity:** called "**Sentinel**" in-game; the player picks a **callsign** right after
  Level 1 and can play as guest or make an account.
- **Kit (start):** a fast, agile ship with a **cannon** (primary) and **rockets** (secondary). Later
  gear is **salvaged** in the field — Machine Gun (after L1), repair drone (after L2). Do **not** give
  the rookie the Machine Gun in the intro; it's the L1 salvage reward.

## Voice (how they narrate — DECISIONS §65)
- **Grounded and a little scared, but holding.** No quips, no bravado, no `!!!`. Beats like "So much
  for easy." and "…Okay. I've got this." — fear that gets mastered, not shrugged off.
- **Competent under pressure, not a savant.** They read the fight and adapt (dodge slow fire, switch
  to rockets when outnumbered, out-turn a missile, then shoot one down) — earned, not effortless.
- **Shows mechanics by doing, not explaining.** The narration reads as a real pilot's inner monologue
  that *happens* to reveal how the tools work — never as a tutorial caption.
